# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local small business owners in Gainesville, GA and the surrounding Hall County area — restaurants, gyms, HVAC companies, contractors, barbers, dentists, food trucks, and other local service businesses. They typically have a bad, outdated, or nonexistent website. They're found via walk-in, cold email, or referral. They're practical people who want a website that works and looks professional — not tech-savvy, not interested in jargon, skeptical of being oversold.

## Product Purpose

lanierweb.com is the sales tool. It converts prospects by demonstrating what good local-business web design looks like — through the quality of the site itself, the demo cards in the Work section, and the prospect mockups linked from pitch conversations. The close happens when a prospect sees a personalized demo of their own business and realizes what they've been missing. The agency site's job is to establish credibility before that conversation happens: real prices, real location, real examples.

## Brand Personality

Confident, grounded, local. Not a tech startup, not a boutique studio. A skilled professional who does exceptional work for real businesses in the community. Voice: clear, direct, no buzzwords. Aesthetic: white, precise, modern — with #FF4500 as the only strong accent. The site should feel like it was built by someone who knows exactly what they're doing.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / Webflow showcase aesthetic — dark backgrounds, glowing cards, floating gradient blobs, neon CTAs, AI-generated feel. Exactly what lanierweb.com must not look like, because it signals "agency that builds for tech startups," not "agency that understands a BBQ restaurant."
- Cheap template agencies (GoDaddy/Wix/Squarespace-style) — stock photography grids, no visual personality, looks like everyone else.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The agency site is itself the portfolio. Every spacing decision, typography choice, and interaction must be an argument for why a prospect should hire Lanier Web.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Demo cards and working mockups close deals faster than feature lists. Lead with evidence, not claims.
3. **Earn trust through specifics.** Real location (Gainesville, GA 30501), real prices ($1,500 / $3,500 / $6,000+), real package names. Specificity signals honesty.
4. **Restrained confidence.** Bold enough to feel premium, clean enough to feel trustworthy, never loud enough to feel like a pitch. One accent, used sparingly.
5. **Mobile-first always.** Target clients check websites on their phones. If it doesn't work on a 390px screen, it doesn't work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort — no formal WCAG target. Ensure readable contrast, keyboard-navigable interactions, and legible type at all sizes. Sufficient for the audience; revisit if the client base expands.
